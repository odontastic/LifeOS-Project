# Module 1: The Theology of Attention

## 1. St. Thomas Aquinas on Solicitude
Aquinas defines **Solicitude** (*solicitudo*) not as worry, but as a virtue related to **Prudence**.
- It is an "alertness of mind."
- It is the opposite of negligence (*negligentia*).
- It is the "anxious care" to ensure that what needs to be done for the good of another is actually done.

**The System Builder's Take**:
Solicitude is the **Watchdog Process** of the soul. It is a background daemon that constantly scans the environment for the needs of the beloved. It is not passive; it is actively polling for status updates.

## 2. St. John Paul II on Original Solitude
In the *Theology of the Body*, JPII teaches that man's first experience was "Original Solitude."
- This was not loneliness (a defect).
- It was the realization of **Subjectivity**: "I am a person, distinct from the animals."
- This solitude was the *necessary prerequisite* for the **Gift of Self**. You cannot give what you do not possess.
