# Area: Pool Maintenance

**Status:** Active
**Standard to Maintain:** Clean, safe, properly balanced pool

---

## GTD Context-Based Task Organization

### @Pool (Physical Pool Work)
- [ ] Pool cleaning (skim, vacuum, brush as needed)
- [ ] Visual inspection of water clarity and equipment
- [ ] Net debris from pool surface
- [ ] Brush pool walls and floor
- [ ] Empty skimmer baskets

### @Computer (Monitoring & Planning)
- [ ] Check pool automation system status
- [ ] Monitor water chemistry readings remotely
- [ ] Research pool maintenance best practices
- [ ] Schedule equipment maintenance appointments
- [ ] Track water usage and chemical consumption

### @Home (Chemical Testing & Equipment)
- [ ] Test chemical levels (pH, chlorine, alkalinity, etc.)
- [ ] Balance chemicals as needed
- [ ] Run filtration and circulation pump
- [ ] Clean and maintain pool filters
- [ ] Inspect pool equipment for wear/damage

### @Shopping (Supplies & Parts)
- [ ] Purchase pool chemicals when needed
- [ ] Order replacement parts for equipment
- [ ] Buy maintenance supplies (nets, brushes, etc.)
- [ ] Research and purchase pool automation upgrades

### @Errands (Professional Services)
- [ ] Schedule professional pool cleaning if needed
- [ ] Arrange equipment repair services
- [ ] Coordinate with pool service companies
- [ ] Pick up special-ordered parts

---

## Ongoing Responsibilities

### Daily/Almost Daily
- Pool cleaning (skim, vacuum, brush as needed)
- Visual inspection
- Maintain cleanliness

### Weekly (Weekends - Discounted Electricity)
- Check chemical levels (pH, chlorine, alkalinity, etc.)
- Balance chemicals as needed
- Test water quality
- Run filtration and circulation pump during discounted hours

### Periodic
- Filter cleaning and maintenance
- Equipment inspection
- Seasonal care

---

## Standards
- Pool clean and clear daily
- Chemical levels balanced weekly
- Safe for swimming
- Cost-effective (use discounted electricity rates when possible)

---

## Schedule
- **Daily cleaning:** As needed (almost daily)
- **Chemical testing/balancing:** Weekend (discounted electricity rates)
- **Pump operation:** Coordinate with discounted electricity windows

---

## Notes
- Weekend chemical maintenance saves on electricity costs
- Daily cleaning prevents major problems
- Safety is priority (proper chemical balance)
