# Initial Coaching Assessment

**Date**: 2025-11-20

## 1. The "Small Thing" Conflict
**Question**: What is the single most common "small thing" you forget that has led to a conflict in the past 48 hours?
**Answer**: My wife got mad at me for not cleaning up the kitchen as I am cooking, like she does. Not organizing and stacking the dirty utensils, dishes, and plates in the sink (note: we are not talking about pre-washing, scrubbing, or cleaning, just making it look neat!)

**Analysis (LifeOS)**:
- **Risk Signal**: To a detail-oriented, risk-averse person (Wife), a chaotic sink signals *loss of control* and *disrespect for the shared environment*. It says "I don't see the work you do to keep order."
- **Pattern**: "Clean as you go" is a process pattern. The conflict isn't about the dishes; it's about the *visual chaos* triggering her anxiety about order/stability.
- **Virtue Opportunity**: **Orderliness** (a sub-virtue of Temperance) and **Service**.

## 2. Emotional Attunement Baseline
**Question**: On a scale of 1 to 10, how confident are you in your ability to recognize and name your wife's underlying feeling when she is becoming anxious?
**Answer**: Maybe low medium, 3 or 4? I'm not sure, but I think I'm a little better.

**Analysis (LifeOS)**:
- **Baseline**: 3.5/10. This is the primary growth area.
- **Gap**: Uncertainty ("I'm not sure") indicates a lack of feedback loops. You don't know if you're right because you might not be checking.
- **Action**: We need to implement "Perception Checking" (e.g., "I'm sensing you're feeling X, is that right?").
