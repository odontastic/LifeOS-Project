# Mental Models for Life & Decision Making

**Top Priority Reminders:**
*   **Inversion:** "What would ruin this?" (Avoid failure > Seek success)
*   **Hanlon's Razor:** "Are they tired/stressed?" (Empathy > Judgment)
*   **Map != Territory:** "The plan is not reality." (Adaptability > Rigidity)

**Purpose:** A latticework of mental models tailored to your specific profile (INTP, Maker, Family-Focused) to help you make better decisions, solve problems, and navigate life's complexities.

---

## 1. Core Thinking Models (The Munger Essentials)

### **Inversion**
*   **The Concept:** Instead of trying to solve a problem forward ("How do I succeed?"), solve it backward ("What would cause me to fail?"). Charlie Munger's favorite: "Tell me where I'm going to die so I will never go there."
*   **Why it fits you:** You are currently navigating complex crises (financial, marital). It is often easier to identify "disaster scenarios" than "perfect solutions."
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Marriage:** Instead of "How do I make my wife happy today?", ask "What would absolutely ruin her day or make her feel unloved?" -> *Don't do that.*
    *   **Business:** Instead of "How do I make a million dollars?", ask "What would guarantee I make $0 and waste my time?" (e.g., never shipping, getting distracted) -> *Avoid those behaviors.*

### **Circle of Competence**
*   **The Concept:** Know the boundary of what you know and what you don't. Success comes from staying within your circle and expanding it slowly, not from stepping outside it.
*   **Why it fits you:** You are a skilled maker/woodworker and system builder. You often feel like an underdog. Your strength lies in *your* specific craft, not in playing someone else's game.
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Career:** Focus on projects where your unique "Maker" skills give you an unfair advantage. Don't try to compete in areas where you are a novice against experts.
    *   **Outsourcing:** If a task (e.g., complex accounting, legal) falls outside your circle, seek help or simplify it rather than struggling to master it immediately.

### **The Map is Not the Territory**
*   **The Concept:** A map (your plan, system, or belief) is a reduction of reality, not reality itself. Reality is messier and more complex.
*   **Why it fits you:** You are an "Architect" (INTP) who loves building systems and plans. You often get frustrated when reality (emotions, family dynamics) doesn't fit your logical system.
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Expect Deviation:** When your day doesn't go according to your "LifeOS" schedule, realize the map was just a guide, not a law. Adapt to the terrain (a crying child, a tired spouse) without feeling like the system failed.

## 2. Productivity & Execution Models

### **The Flywheel Effect**
*   **The Concept:** Greatness isn't one big push; it's the accumulation of small pushes on a giant flywheel. Eventually, it spins with its own momentum.
*   **Why it fits you:** You struggle with consistency and "turning inspiration into bankable output." You need to see that small, daily actions (Atomic Habits) are just pushing the flywheel.
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Content Creation:** Don't look for the "viral hit." Look for the daily 30-minute session. That is one push. Keep pushing, and the momentum will eventually take over.

### **Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)**
*   **The Concept:** 80% of effects come from 20% of causes.
*   **Why it fits you:** You have limited time and energy. You cannot do everything.
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Business:** Which 20% of your products or activities generate 80% of your joy or income? Focus there.
    *   **Family:** What are the 20% of activities that create 80% of the connection with your kids? (e.g., maybe it's just bedtime reading, not expensive trips).

### **Compound Interest**
*   **The Concept:** Small, consistent investments yield massive results over time. This applies to money, habits, relationships, and knowledge.
*   **Why it fits you:** You are building a "Second Brain" and a new life system. It won't pay off in day 1. It pays off in Year 5.
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Relationships:** A small act of kindness every day compounds into a strong marriage. A small neglect every day compounds into divorce.

## 3. Emotional & Relational Models

### **Hanlon's Razor**
*   **The Concept:** "Never attribute to malice that which is adequately explained by stupidity" (or exhaustion, hunger, misunderstanding).
*   **Why it fits you:** In marital conflict, it's easy to feel attacked. This model protects you from spiraling into resentment.
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Conflict:** When your spouse says something hurtful, pause. Ask: "Is she trying to hurt me, or is she just stressed/tired/communicating poorly?" It changes your reaction from defense to empathy.

### **First Principles Thinking**
*   **The Concept:** Break a problem down to its basic truths (physics, biology, facts) and build up from there, rather than reasoning by analogy ("doing what everyone else does").
*   **Why it fits you:** You are a thinker. You don't like following the herd.
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Gender/Identity:** Don't just accept societal narratives. Break down what *you* truly feel, what is biologically true, what is spiritually true for you, and build your identity from those foundational blocks.

### **Regret Minimization Framework**
*   **The Concept:** Project yourself to age 80. Will you regret *not* doing this?
*   **Why it fits you:** You are making big life choices (career, identity, family).
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Decisions:** "In 20 years, will I regret spending this weekend working, or will I regret missing my son's game?" The answer usually becomes clear.

## 4. Strategy Models

### **Opportunity Cost**
*   **The Concept:** The cost of something is what you give up to get it.
*   **Why it fits you:** You have a lot of ideas. You can't do them all.
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Time Management:** "If I spend 2 hours on this low-value task, I am *paying* 2 hours of potential family time or high-value creative work." Is it worth the price?

### **Surfing (Riding the Wave)**
*   **The Concept:** (Another Mungerism) Catch a wave that is already moving. It's easier to succeed if you position yourself where the momentum is.
*   **Why it fits you:** You are looking for business success.
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Trends:** What waves are happening in the maker/woodworking space? (e.g., minimalist desk setups, eco-friendly toys). Position your boat there rather than paddling against the current.

## 5. Biblical Mental Models (Wisdom Literature)

### **Sowing and Reaping (Causality with Delay)**
*   **The Concept:** "For whatever a man sows, that he will also reap." (Galatians 6:7). Actions have consequences, but there is often a *time delay* between the action (sowing) and the result (reaping).
*   **Why it fits you:** You are building long-term projects (LifeOS, family culture). You won't see the fruit immediately.
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Patience:** When you feel like your efforts (in therapy, in business) aren't working, remember the delay. You are sowing seeds. The harvest comes later. Don't dig up the seeds to check if they are growing.

### **Stewardship (The Fiduciary Mindset)**
*   **The Concept:** You are not the *owner* of your life, talents, or family; you are a *manager* (steward) entrusted with them for a time. (Parable of the Talents).
*   **Why it fits you:** It shifts the burden from "I must create success for *my* glory" to "I must manage these resources well for *God's* glory."
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Resource Management:** View your energy, money, and time as capital entrusted to you. Are you burying it (fear/inaction) or investing it (taking calculated risks for growth)?

### **Count the Cost (Strategic Foresight)**
*   **The Concept:** "For which of you, desiring to build a tower, does not first sit down and count the cost, whether he has enough to complete it?" (Luke 14:28).
*   **Why it fits you:** As a system builder, you love starting new projects. This model forces you to evaluate *finish-ability*.
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Project Selection:** Before adding a new "LifeOS" feature or woodworking project, ask: "Do I have the time/energy to *finish* this?" If not, don't start. Unfinished towers are just clutter.

### **The Body (Interdependence & Specialization)**
*   **The Concept:** The eye cannot say to the hand, "I have no need of you." (1 Corinthians 12). Different parts have different functions but the same purpose.
*   **Why it fits you:** You often try to do everything yourself (lone wolf).
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Delegation/Teamwork:** Recognize that your wife, your kids, and your mentors have different strengths. You don't need to be the "hand" if you are the "eye." Lean on their strengths where you are weak (e.g., emotional intuition).

### **The Narrow Gate (Essentialism)**
*   **The Concept:** "Enter by the narrow gate... For the gate is narrow and the way is hard that leads to life." (Matthew 7:13-14). The popular, easy path often leads to mediocrity or destruction. Excellence and virtue require a harder, more selective path.
*   **Why it fits you:** You want "maximum potential." That requires saying "no" to the broad path of distractions.
*   **How to use it:**
    *   **Focus:** When faced with a choice between "easy/popular" and "hard/right," the hard path is usually the one that leads to the life you want.
