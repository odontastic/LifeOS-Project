## The Anger Breakdown

**Surface level**: "She's wasting time organizing instead of just cleaning"

**Deeper level**:

2. **Dismissal of her need**: "This is stupid" = "Your need for visual order is invalid"
3. **Resentment of her standards**: "She just has very high standards despite marrying me"
4. **Feeling controlled**: "She complains to me... about it"

## The Compassionate Reframe

### What If You're Both Right?

**Your perspective**: Organizing before cleaning is inefficient
**Her perspective**: Visual chaos is intolerable

**Both are valid.**

**The question is NOT**: "Who's right?"
**The question IS**: "How do we honor both needs?"

### Possible Solutions:

**Option 1 - Your Way Wins**:
- You clean dishes immediately, no organizing
- Pro: Efficient
- Con: She lives with visual chaos until you clean (anxiety spike)

**Option 2 - Her Way Wins**:
- You organize first, then clean
- Pro: Reduces her anxiety immediately
- Con: Takes more time, feels inefficient to you

**Option 3 - Hybrid**:
- You organize AS you clean (mise en place)
- Pro: Addresses both needs
- Con: Requires you to learn new habit

**Option 4 - Conversation**:
- Ask her: "Help me understand why organizing matters to you"
- Listen without judging
- Find compromise together
